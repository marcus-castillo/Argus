                BRIEF IN SUPPORT OF PLAINTIFF'S MOTION

                          INTRODUCTION

This brief addresses the deprivation of Plaintiff's constitutional rights
under color of state law. As the Supreme Court held in Brown v. Board of
Education, 347 U.S. 483 (1954), state-sponsored segregation violates the Equal
Protection Clause. Plaintiff brings this action pursuant to 42 U.S.C. § 1983,
which provides a cause of action against state actors who deprive individuals
of federally protected rights.

                          ARGUMENT

I.   THE EXCLUSIONARY RULE BARS THE CHALLENGED EVIDENCE

The exclusionary rule applies to the states. See Mapp v. Ohio, 367 U.S. 643
(1961). Moreover, the right to counsel attaches in state felony prosecutions.
Gideon v. Wainwright, 372 U.S. 999, established this principle, and the Court
reaffirmed the protections recognized in Miranda v. Arizona, 384 U.S. 436
(1966).

II.  DUE PROCESS REQUIRES ADDITIONAL PROCEDURES

Procedural due process is governed by the balancing test of Mathews v.
Eldridge, 424 U.S. 319 (1976). The privacy interest at issue also implicates
Roe v. Wade, 410 U.S. 113 (1971), and the agency's interpretation is entitled
to deference under Chevron U.S.A. Inc. v. NRDC, 467 U.S. 837 (1984).

III. ADDITIONAL AUTHORITY

Defendant's reliance on Wilkinson v. Marlborough Capital, 812 F.3d 4471 (9th
Cir. 2016), is misplaced. The controlling rule is instead found in Hampton v.
Sterling Industries, 999 Z.3d 1234 (2050). See also Doe v. Roe, 600 U.S. 1
(2099). Plaintiff further relies on 99 U.S.C. § 9999 and 40 C.F.R. § 261.3.

                          CONCLUSION

For the foregoing reasons, Plaintiff respectfully requests that the Court grant
the motion.
